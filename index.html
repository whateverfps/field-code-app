Chief Building Inspector & Health Officer SOP (Full Document)

Mission & Values: The Chief Building Inspector & Health Officer safeguards Claremont by enforcing codes fairly, protecting public safety, educating the community, and coordinating with departments to ensure a thriving city.

Introduction

As Chief, Paul Thibodeau uses his background to enforce codes, resolve conflicts, and modernize operations. This SOP outlines procedures for inspections, hazard management, abatements, and collaboration.

Section 1 – Role & Legal Authority

Authority under RSA 155-A (Building Code), RSA 147 (Public Health), and RSA 128 (Health Administration). Powers include issuing permits, inspections, stop-work orders, abatements, closures, and coordinating with Police, Fire, DPW, Planner, and City Attorney.

Section 2 – Complaint Handling Workflow

  1. Receive and log complaint.
  2. Investigate: 48 hours (hazard), 72 hours (non-critical).
  3. Document findings.
  4. Resolve via education, abatement, or hazard protocol.
  5. Follow-up and close with report.

Section 3 – Hazard & Imminent Danger Protocol

  1. Evacuate occupants.
  2. Post "Unsafe to Occupy" notices.
  3. Notify Police and Fire.
  4. Issue RSA-cited written order.
  5. Follow up within 48 hours.

Section 4 – Routine Inspections

  • Building permits (construction, mechanical, plumbing, electrical).
  • Rental housing habitability.
  • Restaurant & lodging sanitation.

Section 5 – Abatement & Enforcement

  1. Inspection triggered by complaint or observation.
  2. Investigation and documentation.
  3. Abatement Order issued (deadline, RSA 147/155-A).
  4. Follow-up inspection.
  5. If unresolved: City abatement or closure, costs billed to owner.
  6. Final inspection and documentation.

Section 6 – Coordination Protocols

Work directly with Police (access, security), Fire (life safety), DPW (utilities, drainage), City Planner (zoning compliance), and City Attorney (legal enforcement).

Section 7 – RSA Appendix

RSA 155-A: Enforces IBC, IPC, IMC, IECC, NEC; permits, inspections, stop-work, vacate unsafe buildings.
RSA 147: Public health nuisances, abatement, closures.
RSA 128: Health Officer duties and DHHS coordination.

Section 8 – Leadership & Deputy Management

Chief oversees policy and legal enforcement; Deputy handles routine inspections, intake, and follow-ups. Daily 5-minute check-ins, weekly 15-minute reviews, and Deputy certification path.

Section 9 – Media & Future Growth

Only confirm basic facts, defer details to City Manager. As the city grows, expand staff and shift the Chief toward strategic oversight and Deputy supervisory roles.

Appendix A – Notice Templates

Includes Abatement Orders, Unsafe to Occupy postings, and Business Closure Orders (fillable templates).

Appendix B – First 90 Days Plan

Align department, launch workflows, train Deputy, and deliver a 90-day report to City Manager.

Appendix C – First Staff Meeting Agenda

Outlines introductions, workflow training, communication plans, and Deputy certification track.

Appendix D – Public Overview

Summarizes Chief Thibodeau’s role and services for residents, businesses, and developers with City Hall contact info.
